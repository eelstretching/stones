Sun Microsystems unveils network computer.

Sun Microsystems Inc. said Tuesday it introduced its first network
computer, called the JavaStation.  The scaled-down personal computer,
designed to access corporate networks and the Internet, carries a
price tag of $742, the company said.  Sun said the entry level model,
containing eight megabytes of memory, will ship beginning in
December. A fully configured system, which includes memory, keyboard,
a mouse and fourteen-inch monitor will cost $995.  The company also
said it will offer a JavaStation with 16 megabytes of memory for
$1,565.
